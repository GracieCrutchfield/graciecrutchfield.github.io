Lululemon Advertising Strategy Development

Lululemon is providing an experience of living a healthier lifestyle through their products that accommodate a wide variety of consumers.

Strategy Basics

The Firm

Goal: Help people live longer and healthier lives

Value: Go beyond mediocrity

Resources + Capabilities: Able to innovate technology that lasts

The Environment

Culture of wearing athleisure everywhere and anywhere

The Strategy

Creating a brand that provides consumers with an experience of growth and longevity

Market Position

Industry Leader: Renowned for quality and innovation in the athleisure segment, positioning itself as a lifestyle brand rather than just an apparel retailer

Brand Awareness: Strong engagement through strategic marketing initiatives, ambassador programs, and community events

“The Power to Reimagine Masculinity”

Target Consumer: Justin

Demographics: 50 year old father of two children in college, CMO of a tech company with $150k annual salary, resides in Greenwich, CT

Lifestyle: Equinox member, golfs, Apple Watch daily step tracker, shops at Whole Foods & Under Armour

Psychographics: masculine & head of the family

Objective: Convert 20% of consistent, “macho” Under Armour dad customers, like Justin, to switch and try Lululemon in the next 6 months.

Issue: Dads believe Lululemon will never be a brand for men and prefer more masculine brands, like Under Armour.

Insight: “Lululemon doesn’t have a bro vibe”

Challenge: Transition confident, masculine men like Justin from being consistent Under Armour users to trying Lululemon clothing.
